Golden-bristle Grass

Setaria sphacelata

A tufted grass with narrow, blue-green leaves. Tall golden inflorescences are seen from October to May. Habitat: Grassland; wetland.

  • 1. Seed Production: The large, golden seed heads (panicles) are a critical food source for granivorous birds, including the Long-tailed Widowbird, waxbills, and weavers.
  • 2. An essential larval host plant for several butterfly species, primarily within the Skipper (Hesperiidae) and Brown (Satyrinae) families.
  • 3. Bridge between terrestrial and wetland habitats. Provides essential shelter and breeding grounds for a vast array of wetland-dependent insects, which form the base of the habitat's food web. Helps to filter water of harmful chemicals.
